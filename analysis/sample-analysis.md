# Analysis — 2026-03-13

## Key Findings

The research confirms three things clearly:

First, the gap is real. No product on Gumroad teaches multi-model
coordination without coding. Every competitor assumes a single model.
The closest — AI Systems for Solopreneurs at $27 — still requires
Zapier and third-party tools. We have an open lane.

Second, buyer frustration is documented and widespread. Reddit threads
across three subreddits show the same pattern — people are already
doing this manually, they find it clunky, and they're asking for a
better system. The demand exists before the product does.

Third, the technical competition validates the concept but serves
the wrong buyer. OpenBrain's Supabase/MCP/Vercel stack proves
multi-model coordination works. Their comment section proves
non-technical buyers want it and can't use what exists. That
is our exact buyer.

---

## Competitive Position

We are not competing with prompt libraries. That category is
saturated and priced too low to matter. We are competing with
the idea that multi-model coordination requires technical setup.

Our differentiator in one sentence: the same result, with a free
GitHub account instead of three configured services.

---

## Pricing Recommendation

$29.99. Rationale:

Products with "system" or "workflow" framing command $24-$34 on
Gumroad. Middle of that range is defensible without overreaching.
The $34.99 AI Productivity Bundle is a bundle — three guides sold
together. Our single focused product sits correctly below that.
$19.99 and below signals prompt library, which is the wrong category.

Revisit after first 30 days of sales data.

---

## Buyer Language to Use in Product Copy

Research surfaced the exact words buyers use when they describe
what they want. Use these in the Gumroad listing:

- "practical" and "actually works"
- "no fluff"
- "step by step"
- "I could do this today"
- "no coding"
- "without a team"
